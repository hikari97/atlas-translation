# TASK-0013 — Implement Double Click

## Summary

Implement the `DoubleClickInteraction`, a reusable abstraction responsible for recognizing two consecutive click interactions occurring within a configurable time and distance threshold.

The Double Click Interaction is built on top of the Click Interaction and provides a normalized representation of double-click behavior across supported platforms while remaining independent from rendering, editor logic, and application-specific behavior.

The component is responsible only for identifying a completed double-click interaction. It does not perform selection, editing, command execution, or UI updates.

---

# Goal

Provide a reusable, extensible, and framework-independent double-click interaction abstraction capable of representing double-click lifecycles through a stable public API.

---

# Background

Double-click interactions are commonly used throughout editors.

Typical examples include:

- Opening documents
- Renaming objects
- Editing text
- Expanding tree nodes
- Opening panels
- Activating custom objects

Instead of allowing every subsystem to implement its own double-click detection, Atlas Studio should expose a unified Double Click Interaction abstraction.

# Responsibilities

The Double Click Interaction is responsible for:

- Detecting completed double-click interactions.
- Validating click timing.
- Validating click distance.
- Providing immutable interaction metadata.
- Supporting configurable thresholds.

The Double Click Interaction is **not** responsible for:

- Executing commands.
- Selecting objects.
- Editing documents.
- Rendering.
- Business logic.
