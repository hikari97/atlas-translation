# TASK-0032 — Implement Pan Gesture

## Summary

Implement the `PanGesture`, a reusable abstraction responsible for representing continuous translation gestures within Atlas Studio.

The Pan Gesture recognizes directional movement from one or more active pointers and exposes normalized translation data that can be consumed by higher-level systems such as the editor, workspace, viewport, and plugins.

The Pan Gesture is responsible only for describing gesture state and movement metadata. It does not scroll viewports, move objects, update layouts, or execute application logic.

---

# Goal

Provide a reusable, extensible, and framework-independent pan gesture abstraction capable of representing translation gestures through a stable public API.

---

# Background

Pan is one of the most frequently used gestures in graphical applications.

Typical examples include:

- Canvas panning
- Viewport navigation
- Moving selections
- Moving floating panels
- Touch scrolling
- Trackpad panning
- Plugin-defined navigation

Rather than allowing every subsystem to interpret pointer movement independently, Atlas Studio should expose a unified Pan Gesture abstraction.

# Responsibilities

The Pan Gesture is responsible for:

- Detecting pan movement.
- Calculating translation vectors.
- Tracking gesture lifecycle.
- Providing immutable movement metadata.
- Supporting future pan implementations.

The Pan Gesture is **not** responsible for:

- Scrolling viewports.
- Moving objects.
- Rendering animations.
- Executing commands.
- Business logic.

# Design Principles

The Pan Gesture should be:

- Framework independent
- Immutable
- Strongly typed
- Lightweight
- Extensible
