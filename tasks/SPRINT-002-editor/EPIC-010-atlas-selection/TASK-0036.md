# TASK-0036 — Implement Selection History

## Summary

Implement the `SelectionHistory`, a reusable abstraction responsible for recording and restoring selection states within Atlas Studio.

The Selection History abstraction provides a deterministic history model for tracking selection changes over time. It enables navigation through previous and future selection states while remaining independent from rendering, document mutation, undo/redo systems, and editor-specific implementations.

The Selection History abstraction is responsible only for storing immutable snapshots of selection state and exposing history navigation APIs. It does not modify document contents, execute commands, or implement business logic.

---

# Goal

Provide a reusable, extensible, and framework-independent selection history abstraction capable of recording and restoring selection states through a stable public API.

---

# Background

Professional editors commonly preserve selection history independently from document history.

Typical examples include:

- Previous selection
- Next selection
- Restore selection
- Jump to recently selected objects
- Navigation history
- Plugin-defined history providers

Rather than requiring each subsystem to maintain its own selection history, Atlas Studio should expose a unified Selection History abstraction.

# Responsibilities

The Selection History abstraction is responsible for:

- Recording immutable selection snapshots.
- Navigating backward through history.
- Navigating forward through history.
- Enforcing configurable history limits.
- Supporting future history providers.

The Selection History abstraction is **not** responsible for:

- Undoing document edits.
- Rendering history views.
- Executing commands.
- Persisting history.
- Business logic.
