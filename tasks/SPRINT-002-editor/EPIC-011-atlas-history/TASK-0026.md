# TASK-0026 — Implement History Serializer

## Summary

Implement the `HistorySerializer`, the central abstraction responsible for serializing immutable history models into portable representations within Atlas Studio.

The History Serializer converts complete history structures—including branches, timelines, nodes, transactions, snapshots, and metadata—into deterministic serialized formats suitable for persistence, synchronization, backup, diagnostics, and future collaborative editing.

The History Serializer is responsible only for serialization. It does not restore history, manage storage, execute undo/redo operations, render user interfaces, or implement business logic.

---

# Goal

Provide a reusable, extensible, and framework-independent serialization abstraction capable of exporting complete immutable history structures through a stable public API.

---

# Background

Professional editors frequently serialize history for numerous purposes.

Typical examples include:

- Session persistence
- Crash recovery
- Cloud synchronization
- Autosave
- Export history
- Plugin-defined persistence

Instead of allowing each subsystem to serialize history independently, Atlas Studio should expose a unified History Serializer abstraction.

# Responsibilities

The History Serializer is responsible for:

- Serializing complete history models.
- Producing deterministic output.
- Preserving immutable metadata.
- Supporting serialization formats.
- Supporting future serializer providers.

The History Serializer is **not** responsible for:

- Restoring history.
- Persisting serialized data.
- Compressing history.
- Rendering history.
- Business logic.
