# TASK-0015 — Implement History Limit

## Summary

Implement the `HistoryLimit`, a reusable abstraction responsible for enforcing configurable history capacity within Atlas Studio.

The History Limit abstraction manages the maximum number of immutable history entries that may be retained by the history system. It provides deterministic capacity management while preserving history consistency, transaction integrity, and predictable memory usage. The implementation remains completely independent from rendering engines, persistence layers, application frameworks, and editor implementations.

The History Limit abstraction is responsible only for capacity management. It does not record history, perform undo or redo operations, persist history, or implement business logic.

---

# Goal

Provide a reusable, extensible, and framework-independent history limit abstraction capable of managing immutable history capacity through a stable public API.

---

# Background

Professional editors typically enforce configurable history limits to maintain predictable resource usage.

Typical examples include:

- Maximum history entries
- Transaction-aware trimming
- Automatic history pruning
- Capacity validation
- Configurable retention policies
- Plugin-defined capacity strategies

Instead of allowing each subsystem to manage history capacity independently, Atlas Studio should expose a unified History Limit abstraction.

# Responsibilities

The History Limit abstraction is responsible for:

- Managing maximum history capacity.
- Validating history size.
- Determining removable history entries.
- Supporting configurable capacity policies.
- Preserving deterministic capacity behavior.

The History Limit abstraction is **not** responsible for:

- Recording history.
- Executing undo or redo.
- Persisting history.
- Rendering history.
- Business logic.
