# TASK-0022 — Implement History Timeline

## Summary

Implement the `HistoryTimeline`, a reusable abstraction responsible for representing the chronological sequence of immutable history entries within Atlas Studio.

The History Timeline abstraction provides deterministic chronological navigation across history entries and branches. It maintains ordering, temporal metadata, and navigation semantics while remaining completely independent from rendering engines, persistence layers, editor implementations, and application frameworks.

The History Timeline abstraction is responsible only for representing chronological history. It does not record history, execute undo or redo operations, merge branches, render user interfaces, or implement business logic.

---

# Goal

Provide a reusable, extensible, and framework-independent timeline abstraction capable of representing immutable history chronology through a stable public API.

---

# Background

Professional editing applications frequently expose a chronological history timeline.

Typical examples include:

- Sequential history navigation
- Timeline visualization
- Branch-aware chronology
- Time-based history lookup
- Session replay
- Plugin-defined timeline providers

Rather than relying solely on history indices, Atlas Studio should expose a unified History Timeline abstraction.

# Responsibilities

The History Timeline abstraction is responsible for:

- Maintaining chronological ordering.
- Providing timeline traversal.
- Managing timeline metadata.
- Supporting branch-aware timelines.
- Supporting future timeline providers.

The History Timeline abstraction is **not** responsible for:

- Recording history.
- Merging branches.
- Executing undo or redo.
- Rendering timelines.
- Business logic.
